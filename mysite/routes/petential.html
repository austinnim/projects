Petential

Team: The Mighty Design Rangers

Team Members: Lillian Lee, Elmer Maldonado, Kelly Mayeda, Lereanne Mendoza, Austin Nim, and Emi Oda

Team Values: Intuitive, Accessibility, Visually Aesthetic, Functional, Efficiency

Role: UX/UI Designer, Researcher

Description: This was a 10 week collaborative design project alongside 5 other UCSD students to examine a design context in order to find real world problems that can be explored through technological and representational opportunites that demonstrate a strong understanding of users and their actions. The context chosen to design within were animal shelters in San Diego, with the goal of improving the well-being of animals located in animal shelters. Initial research revolved around interviews and contextual inquiry to identify notable problems. Once data was gathered, models and diagrams were constructed to illustrate the interactivity between stakeholders and aspects of animal shelters. At the end of the 10 weeks, we were able to construct an interactive website prototype that detailed the functionality of the website.

Design process below.

Stakeholders

Primary - Potential adopters and pet owners

Potential adopters and owners are an essential component to the pet adoption process. They are able to provide a consumer perspective and can identify possible benefits or falacies within the steps towards pet ownership. In addition, they provide unique experiences in their journey toward ownership that provides information on the imperatives that lead to the users decision. Potential adoptees and owners have a great impact upon the animals finding a permanent home, thereby increasing the well-being of animals.

Secondary - Employees and volunteers

Employees and volunteers have deep understanding of the animals well-being through direct interactions. In addition, they work within the context and are able to provide knowledge of operations within the shelter and the organizations. This provides an understanding of animal treatment within shelters and the tendencies among potential owners noticed by workers. Moreover, they have a direct impact upon the validity of the solution to determine the potential benefits.

Tertiary - Pet Owners

Pet Owners provide context about the journey with their pets. This is important to understand the unpredictable highs and lows of pet ownership. In addition, they provide knowledge about unforseen events prior to either adoption or ownership. Ultimately, their experiences are valuable in understanding what needs to be done in order to become a pet owner.

The Design Problem

After visiting the San Diego Humane Society, Paws of Coronado, and Rancho Coastal Humane Society the issues centered around owner returns and overpopulation. Interviews with primary and tertiary stakeholders shed light upon differences in adoption process, lack of information, and inaccessibility. In order to tackle the collective as a whole we finalized the design problem towards a way to standardize the adoption process as well as educate users in pet ownership. In return this would result in reduction of owner returns and increased rate of interest for animals within shelters.

Diagrams and Models

After inital inquiry, the team sat down together and consolidated interviews and field observations to create an affinity diagram. This allowed us to group specific aspects together revealing deeper ties.
